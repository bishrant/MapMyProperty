This toolbox contains a tool to determine a minimum diameter for a culvert at a particular point along a stream.

Culvert size is determined from the Minimum Culvert Sizing Chart provided in the Texas Forestry Best Management Practices Manual.   Three factors go in to determining this culvert diameter: (1) area drained in acres, (2) soil texture (light, medium, and heavy), and (3) slope class (flat <5%, moderate 5-15%, steep >15%).  The tool calculates the area in acres that are drained to a “pour-point” that is within 30 meters of the user-defined point and shows on the map the area that is drained (its watershed).

To calculate a culvert size, click on the Draw Point button and click a point on the map along a drainage.  It may be useful to refer to the topo map when doing this.  After a moment, the watershed will appear along with the user-defined point and the calculated pour point.  The purpose of the pour point, which is the lowest area within 30 meters of the user-defined point, is to minimize anomalies for calculating the watershed size when locating a point for a culvert.
