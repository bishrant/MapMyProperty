Welcome to Plan My Land Operation!

This tour will introduce you to the tools in this application.  For detailed information, click the ? button, located on the top right of each toolbox.  You can always open this tour again by clicking in the "Help" link located in the upper right corner of the application.

Find Location

Use the Find Location tool to locate addresses, place names and coordinates in decimal degrees.

Map Navigation

You can use your mouse to pan and your scroll wheel to zoom.  Gestures work for navigation on touch devices.

You can also use the map buttons to zoom to Texas, zoom in, and zoom out.

Draw

Draw toolbox contains tools to draw and edit areas, circles, lines and points of interest.  In addition, text can be added.

Sensitive Areas

Sensitive Areas toolbox allows you to display various sensitive areas within a project area from a context of Best Management Practices (BMPs) for the protection of soil and water quality.  You can also create a report with a map and a summary of the sensitive area types.

Soils

In the Soils toolbox you can access to several soil attributes directly from the Soil Survey Geographic (SSURGO) Database.  You can also clip the soil polygons within your project area and create a full report with all the soils information.

Harvest Operations

Harvest Operations toolbox allows you to see selected attributes such as Harvest Equipment Operability from the Soil Survey Geographic (SSURGO) Database.  You can change the attribute you want to display for you project area and create a summary report.

Regeneration Operations

Regeneration Operations toolbox allows you to see selected attributes such as Potential for Seedling Mortality from the Soil Survey Geographic (SSURGO) Database.  You can change the attribute you want to display for you project area and create a summary report.

Elevation Profile

Elevation Profile toolbox allows you to explore the profile of a drawn line in the map.  You will be able to identify steep slopes and create a summary report of the drawn path.

Culvert Size

The Culvert Size tool works only for Texas and determines the minimum recommended diameter for a culvert based on three factors: (1) acres drained, (2) soils texture, and (3) slope.  Once the tool runs you will be able to generate a summary report of the results.

Import | Export

The Import | Export toolbox allows you to load and save drawings (areas, lines, points of interest, and text) in multiple formats.  This gives you the ability of sharing your drawings with other people.

Export to pdf

The Export to PDF toolbox allows you to create a printable GeoPDF map matching the contents of you map.  You also will be able to add a title and some comments to your map.

Basemaps

You can also choose what basemap to display as the map background.  Selecting the Texas thumbnail will display a dropdown list with options for statewide aerial imagery.

Layers

You can view different information in the map by turning items on and off in the list.

Swipe Layers

You can compare interactively two different imagery layers for the state of Texas.
